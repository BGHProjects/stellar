# Route Types

Four route types are available on qualifying routes. Each represents a different trade-off between speed, cost, and experience.

## Direct Transfer

**Speed:** 1.0× ship class cruise speed  
**Price:** 1.3× base price  
**Scatter risk:** Standard

The fastest available route — point to point, no intermediate stops or trajectory assistance. Premium priced because it requires maximum fuel burn for the entire journey.

Available on Helion, Tethys, and Solaris class vessels. Lunara-class does not offer direct transfer — its operational profile is scenic and gravity assist.

**Best for:** Passengers who need to arrive as soon as possible and price is secondary.

---

## Gravity Assist Route

**Speed:** 0.72× ship class cruise speed  
**Price:** 0.85× base price (15% cheaper than base)  
**Scatter risk:** Reduced

A fuel-efficient trajectory that uses the gravitational pull of intermediate planetary bodies to accelerate the ship without burning propellant. The route is longer in distance but more fuel-efficient, reflected in a lower base price.

The Tethys-class is specifically optimised for gravity assist routing — its fuel systems are designed around the assumption of slingshot trajectories. On longer routes, Gravity Assist on a Tethys frequently costs significantly less than Direct Transfer on the same class.

**Best for:** Passengers with some schedule flexibility who want a lower fare. The slower effective speed (0.72×) means a longer journey, but the cost saving can be substantial on outer-system routes.

---

## Multi-Stop Voyage

**Speed:** 0.60× ship class cruise speed  
**Price:** 0.70× base price (30% cheaper than base)  
**Scatter risk:** Standard

The ship calls at multiple ports across the journey — for resupply, passenger exchange, or because no direct service exists on that route. The cheapest option for long-haul travel.

The significantly longer effective journey time (0.60× speed) means multi-stop is most appropriate for passengers travelling with a cryo option, particularly Full Cryo where the passenger experiences no additional time regardless of stop duration.

**Best for:** Budget-conscious travellers on long-haul routes, especially with Full Cryo.

---

## Scenic Voyage

**Speed:** 0.50× ship class cruise speed  
**Price:** 1.65× base price (65% above base)  
**Scatter risk:** Minimal

A deliberately extended route that deviates to pass notable astrophysical phenomena — the inner edge of the Scatter, Vareth's ring plane, Serrath's terminator line. Exclusive to the Lunara-class.

Scenic voyages are the most expensive option and the slowest. They are sold as an experience, not transport. Passengers booking a Scenic voyage are choosing to spend more time in transit and more money doing so because the route itself is the attraction.

Full Cryo is not available on Scenic voyages — you are expected to be awake for this.

**Best for:** First-time system travellers, passengers with astronomical interests, anyone who wants the journey to be the memory.
