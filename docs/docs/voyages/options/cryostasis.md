# Cryostasis Options

Three cryostasis options are available on cryo-capable vessels (Tethys, Lunara, Solaris). The choice is made before selecting a cabin class, because it determines which amenities and cabins are available for the rest of the booking.

## Conscious Voyage

**Price:** 1.0× (base — cheapest option)  
**Amenities:** Full access  
**Experience:** Every day of the voyage is lived

No cryostasis. The passenger is awake for the entire journey with full access to all ship amenities — dining, recreation, entertainment, observation deck, and any booked add-ons.

On a Solaris-class voyage to Calyx at average orbital separation (192 days), a Conscious Voyage means 192 days of shipboard life. The ship becomes home. Passengers develop routines, relationships, and a relationship with the void outside their viewports that cannot be replicated.

All cabin classes are available. Required for Helix Class — the observation bubble and personal steward are premised on the passenger being awake to use them.

---

## Full Cryo

**Price:** 0.55× (45% less than base — significant saving)  
**Amenities:** None  
**Experience:** Departure one day, arrival the next (subjectively)

The passenger is placed under for the entire voyage, waking on arrival. No cabin amenities apply — food, entertainment, recreation, and most add-ons are disabled. The passenger experiences nothing of the journey.

Full Cryo is practical and significantly cheaper — removing amenity consumption from the equation reduces costs substantially on long voyages. On a 192-day Aethon–Calyx voyage, Full Cryo makes the journey subjectively instantaneous.

Not available on Helix Class or Lunara-class vessels.

:::tip When Full Cryo makes sense
Full Cryo is the right choice when you need to be somewhere and don't care about the journey. It's also the choice that makes the cheapest cabin class (Drift) viable on very long routes — if you're not experiencing the shared amenities, the private cabin premium is irrelevant.
:::

## Cryo Intervals

**Price:** 1.35× (35% above base — most expensive option)  
**Amenities:** Available during conscious windows  
**Experience:** Time compression with curated waking periods

The passenger alternates between cryosleep and conscious waking periods across the voyage. The number of intervals is selected at booking, with a minimum and maximum calculated from journey duration.

| Journey Duration | Minimum Intervals | Maximum Intervals |
| ---------------- | ----------------- | ----------------- |
| 10–30 days       | 1                 | 3                 |
| 30–60 days       | 1                 | 6                 |
| 60–120 days      | 2                 | 12                |
| 120–200 days     | 4                 | 12                |

During conscious windows, full amenity access applies. The Lunara-class uses intervals specifically to offer curated astronomical viewing windows — passengers wake as the ship approaches a notable phenomenon and return to cryo after passing it.

Cryo Intervals is the most expensive option because it is genuinely the most sophisticated technically — the cryo system cycles rather than running continuously, and the waking windows require crew attention and full ship services.

**Best for:** Long-haul passengers who want to experience some of the voyage without committing to the full duration. Also the optimal choice for the Lunara-class where scenic waypoints are the selling point.

## Cryostasis and Vaelisite

The cryostasis gel used in all cryo-capable vessels contains Vaelisite, a mineral mined exclusively from Vael — the coldest inhabited body in the Calyx system. There is a certain irony: the most remote, harshest settlement in the system produces the compound that makes long-distance space travel comfortable for everyone else.
