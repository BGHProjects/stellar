# The Binary Stars

The Taunor system orbits two stars. This shapes everything — the lighting, the seasons, the culture.

## Taunor Prime

A G-type main sequence star, slightly warmer and more orange-hued than Sol. Fixed at the system barycentre for simulation purposes. All orbital periods and radii are measured relative to its position.

**Render colour:** `#FFF4C2` (warm golden-white)

## Taunor Minor

A K-type orange dwarf orbiting Taunor Prime at 0.08 AU on an 18-day period. It is visible from every inhabited body in the system as a second sun — sometimes close to Taunor Prime in the sky, sometimes widely separated, depending on orbital phase.

**Render colour:** `#FFB347` (amber-orange)

## Dual-Sun Effects

**Double shadows** — on Aethon during mid-day, objects cast two distinct shadows when the stars are sufficiently separated in the sky.

**The Pale Season** — on Aethon's equatorial zones, there are periods when both stars are simultaneously above the horizon for 40+ hours continuously. Businesses run through it; higher-latitude residents consider it a reason never to move south.

**Amber twilights** — as Taunor Minor sets independently of Taunor Prime, it creates twilight periods with a distinctive orange-amber cast that is unique to the system.

# The Scatter

The Scatter is a dense belt of asteroids, debris clouds, and uncharted rock occupying 1.8–2.6 AU from the system barycentre — between the inner system (Aethon, Vareth) and the outer system (Calyx).

## Origin

Planet formation in this region was disrupted by the gravitational interplay of the binary stars and the competing pulls of the inner and outer planets. The result is an asteroid belt significantly denser and more chaotic than our own solar system's equivalent.

## Navigation

The Scatter is not a wall. Ships cross it regularly on plotted courses through charted corridors. What it is not safe to do is traverse it on an unplotted course — the risk of debris collision is too high.

The different route types reflect this reality:

- **Gravity Assist routes** thread known stable corridors between asteroid clusters
- **Scenic Voyages** run along the inner edge where the debris thins and the tumbling, illuminated rocks are spectacular
- **Asteroid Deviation Coverage** insurance exists specifically because Scatter crossings carry an elevated risk of uncharted debris

## In the 3D Map

The Scatter renders as a particle field — dense at the core, thinning toward the edges, with distinct charted corridor gaps visible as slightly cleaner paths through the cloud. In the star map, you can see it as the band of small grey objects between the 1.8 AU and 2.6 AU orbital rings.
