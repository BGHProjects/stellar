# Uninhabitable Bodies

Two additional bodies exist in the system — visible in the 3D star map, observable, but not part of the travel network. They appear on the map because the system is more interesting and more realistic with them there.

---

## Serrath

**Type:** Rocky planet  
**Position:** 0.6 AU from Solara Prime — inner system, between the binary stars and Aethon  
**Status:** Uninhabitable  
**Orbital Period:** 145 days

### Why It Cannot Be Inhabited

Serrath sits in what would technically be a habitable zone, but a cascade of geological and atmospheric failures renders it completely uninhabitable.

**Near-total tidal locking.** Serrath's rotation has been almost completely arrested by Vareth's gravitational influence during an earlier period of its orbital history. It rotates so slowly that one side bakes under the binary stars for over 200 local days continuously, reaching temperatures that melt surface rock into flowing lava plains. The other side freezes to hundreds of degrees below zero in permanent darkness.

**Collapsed magnetosphere.** Its iron core solidified prematurely approximately 800 million years ago. Without a magnetosphere, the stellar wind from both binary stars has been stripping its atmosphere for geological timescales. What remains is a thin, toxic wisp of carbon dioxide and sulphur compounds — offering no pressure protection and no radiation shielding.

**The terminator line.** The narrow band between the permanently molten day-side and the frozen night-side is a violent storm wall where superheated atmosphere crashes into the frozen side. This terminator line is one of the great visual spectacles of the inner system — a lava sea and a frozen plain separated by a raging storm, all visible simultaneously.

### As a Destination

Serrath is not a landing destination — there is no spaceport and no safe landing site. However, Lunara-class scenic voyages run a deliberate close-approach flyby every 30 days. Passing within visual range of Serrath's terminator line at safe distance is considered one of the defining sights of a Solara system voyage.

## Drath

**Type:** Gas giant  
**Position:** 9.4 AU from Solara Prime — outer edge of the system  
**Status:** Uninhabitable — no sanctioned habitation of any body in its system  
**Orbital Period:** 6,100 days (~16.7 standard years)

### Why It Cannot Be Inhabited

**Erratic magnetosphere.** Drath's magnetosphere is one of the most powerful and unpredictable in the system. Unlike Vareth's stable shielding — which makes its inner moons viable for colonisation — Drath's field periodically reverses polarity on timescales of decades. During a reversal event, the radiation environment around Drath and all its moons spikes to levels lethal to unprotected biology within hours.

**Unpredictable reversal cycles.** The reversal cycle is not precisely predictable — only statistically modelled. This means no long-term surface habitation has ever been sanctioned on any Drath body. The risk window cannot be reliably calculated far enough in advance.

**Inert moons.** Drath's moons lack the tidal heating that makes Vareth's moons useful. Without internal heat and without stellar warmth at this distance, they are cold, airless, radiation-blasted rock. Several have been surveyed; none have been deemed viable.

### Visual Character

Drath is visually subdued compared to Vareth — a muted blue-grey giant with faint banding, almost featureless. Where Vareth blazes, Drath broods. Its most notable visual feature is the aurora activity generated by its erratic magnetosphere — vast curtains of shifting light in cold blues and greens visible from deep space during polarity disturbance events.

In the 3D star map, Drath sits at the system's edge, slow and dim, a reminder that not everything out there is hospitable. Ships do not route near Drath. It does not appear in the booking system.
