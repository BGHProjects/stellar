# Calyx System

Calyx is a small, dense ice planet in the outer system beyond the Scatter — the furthest inhabited world from the binary stars. It has two colonised moons: Lun and Vael.

---

## Calyx

**Type:** Ice planet  
**Position:** 5.8 AU from Solara Prime  
**Status:** Colonised — 3 spaceports  
**Population:** ~1.8 million  
**Gravity:** 0.8g  
**Atmosphere:** Thin nitrogen — fully pressurised habitats required  
**Temperature:** -190°C to -140°C (surface)  
**Orbital Period:** 2,400 days (~6.3 standard years)

### Overview

Calyx is cold in a way that is difficult to overstate. Surface temperature would kill an unprotected human in minutes. What it lacks in warmth it compensates for in resources — Calyx's crust is extraordinarily mineral-rich, a consequence of the Scatter's debris having rained down on the outer system for billions of years.

The colonists live almost entirely underground or in pressurised surface domes, and have developed a culture that is deeply interior-focused — architecture, art, and social life built around enclosed shared spaces rather than open landscape. The underground city networks are lit by a spectrum of artificial light tuned to maintain circadian rhythms. From orbit at night, the city grids are visible as geometric light patterns through the ice sheet — one of the unexpected wonders of the system.

From the surface, both binary stars are visible simultaneously — but at this distance, they are little more than bright stars in the sky, indistinguishable from each other.

### Spaceports

**Calyx North Port** — The primary northern hemisphere surface port. Connects directly to the largest underground city network.

**Calyx Equatorial Port** — Mid-latitude surface port with the highest traffic volume on Calyx. Primary commercial hub.

**Calyx Deep Station** — A deep-ice port integrated directly into the underground city network. The most sheltered departure point in the system — you board a ship without ever going outside.

## Lun

**Type:** Moon of Calyx  
**Status:** Colonised — 1 spaceport  
**Population:** ~18,000  
**Gravity:** 0.15g  
**Atmosphere:** None — full EVA required  
**Orbital Period:** 9 days (tidally locked)

### Overview

A dark rocky moon with subsurface liquid water pockets. Colonised primarily for its strategic orbital position — ideal for deep-outer-system observation and relay communications. Lun is a listening post as much as a settlement. Its orbital position makes it the best relay node in the outer belt.

The population is mixed — about half scientific and communications personnel, half a civilian community that has grown organically around the support infrastructure. Lun is quieter than anywhere else in the system. The view of Calyx from the surface, pale and vast, has a quality that residents describe as meditative.

## Vael

**Type:** Moon of Calyx  
**Status:** Colonised — 1 spaceport  
**Population:** ~4,200  
**Gravity:** 0.12g  
**Atmosphere:** None — full EVA required  
**Temperature:** -230°C (coldest inhabited surface in the system)  
**Orbital Period:** 17 days (tidally locked)

### Overview

The coldest inhabited body in the system. A high-albedo ice moon that reflects distant binary light more efficiently than Calyx itself. Its colony exists entirely to mine Vaelisite — a naturally occurring compound found in trace amounts across the outer system, but concentrated in Vael's upper crust at commercially viable densities.

Vaelisite is a key component of the cryostasis gel used in virtually every cryo-capable ship in the fleet. There is a certain irony not lost on Vael's colonists: they live in the harshest, most remote inhabited location in the system, extracting the material that makes long-distance space travel comfortable for everyone else.

**Vael Station** — Small, functional mining colony port. The coldest staffed spaceport in the system. Arrivals are greeted with a temperature advisory and a reminder that full EVA gear is required outside pressurised areas at all times.
